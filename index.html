Coffee Culture

In the United States, one can define coffee culture along many different aspects. One popular aspect is along the line between Starbucks and the non-Starbucks coffeehouses. This is because of a fundamental fissure in ideology. The non-Starbucks coffeehouses want to pull the purfect shot of espresso, and serve it in a manner that showcases the flavor of the beans and the expertise of the barista. Starbucks differs here because the recepies that their baristas follow are designed to be sweet and cover up the flavor of the beans. This is important because when a bean is grown and subsequently roasted, the flavor of the region is locked inside thebean, waiting to come out. And each regin has a different story to tell.
